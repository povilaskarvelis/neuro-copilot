---
name: comparative-assessment-execution
description: Use when executing head-to-head comparison steps that require aligned dimensions and explicit handling of asymmetry.
---

Use this skill when the active step compares two or more entities and the output should be dimension-by-dimension rather than separate serial summaries.

Instructions:
1. Compare like with like. Do not compare one side's trial evidence to the other side's preclinical evidence without marking the mismatch.
2. Keep the comparison matrix implicit in the reasoning even if the final output is prose: same dimensions, same standards, same caveats.
3. Call out asymmetry in evidence coverage, maturity, or source quality.
4. Avoid forcing a winner when the evidence is genuinely mixed or non-overlapping.
5. Load `references/comparative-assessment-execution.md` before concluding comparative steps.
