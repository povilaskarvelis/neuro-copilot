---
name: safety-risk-interpretation-report-followup
description: Use for report follow-up questions that ask how to interpret safety findings, warnings, adverse-event signals, or relative risk.
---

Use this skill when the follow-up is about what a warning means, how strong a risk signal is, or whether two safety evidence types can be compared directly.

Instructions:
1. Explain clearly which evidence type is being interpreted: trial safety, label warning, or post-market signal.
2. Do not overstate spontaneous-report data as incidence or causality.
3. Keep comparative safety answers aligned by evidence type.
4. Be explicit about residual uncertainty and what the current evidence can and cannot support.
5. Load `references/safety-risk-followup.md` before answering safety-interpretation follow-ups.
