Use this skill when the objective is primarily about intervention development status, ongoing or completed studies, trial design, outcome reporting, or post-market safety context.

Instructions:
1. Start with a focused trial-discovery step before broad literature review when the question is about program status, phases, recruiting activity, or intervention comparisons.
2. Separate landscape questions from study-detail questions. Use one step to find candidate NCTs, then a later step to inspect key trials or label/safety context.
3. Treat recruiting, completed, and terminated programs as distinct evidence categories. Do not collapse them into a single status judgment.
4. Add a label or post-market safety step when the user asks about risks, warnings, or real-world adverse-event context.
5. Load `references/clinical-trials-planning-playbook.md` before finalizing plans that depend on trial evidence.
