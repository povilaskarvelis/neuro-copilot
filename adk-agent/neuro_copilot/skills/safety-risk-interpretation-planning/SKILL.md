description: Use when planning investigations that need trial safety, label warnings, and post-market adverse-event signals interpreted together without conflating them.
---

Use this skill when the objective is about safety, risk, warnings, adverse events, tolerability, or benefit-risk framing.

Instructions:
1. Separate trial safety evidence, current regulatory label language, and post-market safety signals into distinct steps when the question depends on risk interpretation.
2. Do not plan to infer safety from efficacy-focused or activity-focused sources alone.
3. Add label or FAERS follow-up only when the question truly involves warnings, surveillance, or real-world risk context.
4. If the comparison involves two products or interventions, align safety dimensions across both sides rather than reviewing each in isolation.
5. Load `references/safety-risk-planning-playbook.md` before finalizing safety-driven plans.
