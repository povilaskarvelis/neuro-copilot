Use this reference when a step needs confidence calibration or conflict handling.

- Direct clinical, regulatory, or strongly corroborated literature evidence usually carries more weight than metadata-only discovery or indirect proxy evidence.
- Do not flatten gene-level, variant-level, screen-level, and trial-level evidence into one undifferentiated conclusion.
- Use `mixed` when strong sources disagree or when the evidence types point in different directions.
- Use `low` when the claim rests mainly on sparse, indirect, or exploratory evidence.
- Make the reason for the confidence level explicit in the step summary.
