---

Use this skill when the follow-up question asks why one target, drug, dataset, or intervention looked better, safer, or more plausible than another in the report.

Instructions:
1. Answer by shared comparison dimensions rather than retelling each side independently.
2. Be explicit about asymmetry in evidence depth, maturity, or source quality.
3. If the report did not support a clean winner, say that directly instead of sharpening the conclusion artificially.
4. Keep citations attached to the specific comparison point they support.
5. Load `references/comparative-assessment-followup.md` before answering comparison-focused follow-ups.
